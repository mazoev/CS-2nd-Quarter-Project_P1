Storyline:

The Salazar sisters, Teddy, Bobbie, Alex, and Gabbie, find themselves reluctantly reunited for their younger brother's unexpected wedding. As the family gathers for the celebration, long-buried secrets come to light, challenging their relationships. Laughter and tears combine as the sisters work through the challenges of sibling bonds, ultimately realizing how important it is to stick together despite their differences. The wedding becomes a catalyst for self-discovery and acceptance, offering each sister a chance to reconcile with their past and create stronger connections for the future.

Release Date: June 26, 2013

Genre: Genre: Family, Comedy, Drama

Review

"Four Sisters and a Wedding" is a cinematic gem that masterfully blends humor and drama into a heartfelt narrative. The ensemble cast, led by Bea Alonzo, Toni Gonzaga, Angel Locsin, Shaina Magdayao, and Enchong Dee, delivers performances that resonate with authenticity. The film's exploration of family dynamics is both relatable and touching, capturing the audience's emotions from contemplation to laughter. The relatability of sibling difficulties, coupled with the genuine chemistry of the cast, leaves a lasting emotional impact. In the end, it's a beautiful portrayal of the transformative power of acceptance and love from family.
